    First on the list is Ecology

    • We can start with what life is
      • Important Vocabulary
        • Biotic
          • The living components of an ecosystem
            • ex. bacteria, feathers, bones, hair
            • Anything that is part of an organism or was part of an organism can be considered biotic
        • Abiotic
          • The nonliving components of an ecosystem
            • ex. rocks, fire, air, sunlight
        • Ecosystem
          • An area where different organisms interact with each other.

      • Now onto the real sauce:

        • Characteristics of Living Things
          • Are made of cells.
          • A cell is the smallest living thing.
          • Unicellular or multicellular
            • Unicellular: Single Celled Organisms
              • ex. bacteria
            • Multicellular: Organisms made up of more than one cell
              • ex. humans,bats, whales, fish, etc.
              • Literally anything that's not bacteria/single-celled is either multicelled or not considered an organism
          • Have a genetic codeThis will be covered in higher detail further into the unit.
            • DNA
              • DNA is a polymer. It is composed of a crap tonne of smaller parts called monomers. It is often easier to think of this like a train. A single train can be composed of lots of smaller segments, each individual one being referred to as a monomer.
              • (DNA)Deoxyribonucleic Acid
                • Found in the nucleus of a cell
                • Source code of a cell
                • I'm not sure about y'all, but I'd guess that it's pretty important.
                • Try not to damage it
            • (RNA)Ribonucleic Acid
              • There is more than one type of RNA. These are:
                • mRNA:
                  • Messenger RNA
                  • Copies cell's source code(DNA) for protein synthesis outside cell nucleus
                  • Main Role in Cell: Carry protein blueprints out of nucleus without taking the DNA
